Tarun's expert framework library — 12 frameworks
TARUN ONLY — not applied to Ed or Dan
Chris Voss
Never Split the Difference
Mirror prospect's last 3 words · Label emotions ("It seems like…") · Calibrated questions ("How am I supposed to…?") · No = start of conversation
Jeb Blount
Fanatical Prospecting
Pattern interrupt opener · Bridge pain to solution in 30 sec · One CTA only · Phone > email for breakthrough
Justin Welsh
LinkedIn Content System
Hook in first line or lose them · Specificity beats vague every time · Curiosity gap drives clicks · Short sentences win
Alex Hormozi
$100M Offers
Numbers over adjectives · "20–40 hours saved" not "saves time" · Remove all friction · Make it a no-brainer
Aaron Ross
Predictable Revenue
3–4 sentences cold max · Sell the meeting not the product · Personalization over volume · One ask only
David Ogilvy
Ogilvy on Advertising
First sentence does 80% of the work · Specifics sell · Every sentence earns its place or gets cut
Vanessa Van Edwards
Captivate · Cues · Science of People
Lead with warmth cues before competence · Power of the eyebrow flash in first meeting · Name + interest = instant liking · Ask "What's your biggest challenge with X?" not "Do you have a problem with X?"
Robert Cialdini
Influence · Pre-Suasion
Reciprocity: give value first · Social proof: "Others in your industry..." · Authority: mention CBH + Platinum Partner · Scarcity: "only a few EAP slots" · Liking: find genuine common ground · Unity: "we" language
Dale Carnegie
How to Win Friends & Influence People
Use their name · Be genuinely interested in THEM · Let them do most of the talking · Make them feel important and heard · Never criticize — ask questions instead
Daniel Kahneman
Thinking Fast and Slow
Loss aversion: "stop losing 30 hrs/mo" hits harder than "save 30 hrs" · Anchor high first · Frame as avoiding pain not gaining benefit · System 1 decides — make it feel obvious
Daniel Pink
To Sell Is Human
Attunement: see from their perspective first · Problem finding > problem solving · Clarity: help them see what they can't see · Buoyancy: positive realism, not toxic positivity
Joe Navarro
What Every BODY Is Saying · FBI
Read comfort vs discomfort signals · Feet point toward interest · Pacifying behavior = stress signal · Open palms build trust · Match their energy level to build rapport
How these frameworks apply to your emails
Subject line → Welsh (hook) + Ogilvy (specific)
Opening sentence → Van Edwards (warmth first) + Carnegie (their name + genuine interest)
Pain statement → Kahneman (loss frame: "stop losing 30 hrs/mo") + Hormozi (specific numbers)
Social proof → Cialdini (others in their industry + authority signals)
CTA → Blount (one ask) + Aaron Ross (sell the meeting, not the product)
Tone throughout → Voss (calibrated questions) + Pink (attunement — see from their view)
